Lot

A lot is a listing for the sale of some valuable item, allowing buyers to place bids.

Bid

A bid is the price a potential buyer is willing to offer for the item.

Winner

The winner is the buyer who places the highest bid.

Lot Closure

Lot closure is the end of the bidding process, at which point the winner acquires the item at their bid price.

LOT RULES

LOT-1:
A lot is created with a currency, an end time, and a minimum bid price. After creation, the lot is in the OPEN state.

LOT-2:
Bids can only be placed when the lot is in the OPEN state.

LOT-3:
All bids must be in the same currency as the lot.

LOT-4:
A bid can only be placed if:

the bid is greater than 1.05 × the current highest bid for the lot, or
if there are no bids yet, the bid is greater than or equal to the minimum bid price.

LOT-5:
A lot transitions to the CLOSED state if:

-the end time has expired and at least one bid has been placed, or
-the lot is in the OPEN state and the creator decides to close it early.

LOT-6:
A lot transitions to the DRAW state if:

-the lot is in the OPEN state and the creator cancels it, or
-the end time expires and no bids were placed.

LOT INVARIANTS
-The end time of a lot cannot be earlier than the current time and cannot be later than 6 months from now.
-A lot must always be in one of the following states: OPEN, CLOSED, or DRAW.
